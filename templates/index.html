About

Team Foodtopia

  • March 25th - 28th

    The Journey Begins!

    We embark on our expedition to find the best data set for our project. The journey is long and treacherous as we struggle to find a compatible API that will suit our needs. But alas, success finds us as we stumble upon the long-forgotten Spoonacular API. We revel in data extraction, cleaning, and Jsonifying, as we start to see a clear image for our final goal, we realize the real challenge awaits us…

  • March 29th - April 1st

    The Vision is Clear

    As we have come to familiarize ourselves with this vast sea of data, we set our sights on the visualization aspect of this project. How will we present our discoveries, we ask ourselves. We determine the charts, graphs, and maps that best suit the nature of the dataset, and we get to work. The work is challenging, yet inspiring. As we traverse the challenging terrain of Plotly, the depth of D3, and the impressive heights of Leaflet. We encounter many new discoveries, one of which in the form of a wild Sunburst, an exciting discovery and a welcome change to the stale pie chart. We come one step closer to a culmination of our efforts.

  • April 2nd

    You Are the CSS to my HTML

    With our visions set, we now construct our framework for the final product. We strive for a unified product that will convey our findings to the world. Our path is clear, but the puzzle is immense. After much deliberation, the council came to a conclusion. The culmination of our efforts yields a beautiful prototype (one that will surely grant us an A+ on this project), but rest is yet but a dream, as the journey is not over…

  • April 3rd

    Liftoff

    The end is finally upon us. We draw closer to the deadline and we scramble to fix bugs, optimize performance, and well… make it look good. It all comes together as we hit Go Live one final time. We can finally breathe freely, or so it seems.

  • April 4th

    C'est Fini

    This is it. The day we have been dreaming and also dreading, for what seems like an eternity. We announce our findings to the world and hope that it welcomes us with open arms (and again, that A+ would be nice as well). As we wind down, we reflect on our expedition and draw connections between us and that one famous guy who went by the name Columbus or something like that. We learned much along the way, but the greatest lesson learned was that social distancing isn't all that bad.
